# The Motivation Problem in Digital Work

- Digital work is inherently abstract and intangible
- Staring at blank screens or empty code editors creates mental resistance
- Initial friction makes starting any project feel overwhelming

# AI as a Low-Effort Catalyst

- Asking AI agents for solutions to problems, even with minimal context
- The act of seeing *any* result kicks your brain into gear
- Even poor AI responses provide a starting point to work with

# Related Psychological and Social Concepts

- Cunningham's Law: Posting wrong answers attracts correct solutions
- The power of working inertia - once started, momentum builds
- Breaking through the blank page syndrome

# Outcomes and Benefits

## When AI Succeeds
- Perfect solution eliminates the need for manual work
- Still provides valuable context and learning opportunities

## When AI Falls Short
- Broken or incomplete solutions become tangible problems to fix
- Re-prompting forces deeper thinking about the problem
- Clear visibility into what's wrong helps identify fix steps
- Watching AI struggle creates motivation to intervene and help

## Context Window Benefits
- Crafting good prompts forces you to understand your problem better
- The process of explaining to AI clarifies your own thinking
- Even bad initial prompts ("plz implement") can spark the process

# The Low-Effort On-Ramp

- Minimal effort required to get started with AI assistance
- Any result is better than no result when motivation is low
- Creates a feedback loop of small wins and progress
